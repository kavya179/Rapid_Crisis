✅ WHAT TO DO
✔ Stay calm and press SOS only once — multiple presses create duplicate alerts
✔ Fill in your location and room number so responders can find you quickly
✔ Move to a safe area while waiting for help to arrive
✔ Keep your phone accessible so responders can call you back
✔ Follow evacuation routes if there is fire, gas leak, or structural danger
✔ Call national emergency services (100/101/108) in parallel for life-threatening situations
❌ WHAT NOT TO DO
✖ Do NOT press SOS for non-emergencies or to test the system — it triggers a full response
✖ Do NOT leave the area without telling a responder — they will be searching for you
✖ Do NOT use elevators during fire or earthquake — use stairs only
✖ Do NOT attempt to fight fire, subdue an attacker, or handle hazmat yourself
✖ Do NOT block corridors or exits — keep pathways clear for emergency teams
✖ Do NOT dismiss or close the SOS alert screen until help has confirmed arrival
